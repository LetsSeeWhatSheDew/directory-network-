# The PuffPrice Promise

**Date:** April 18, 2026
**Owner:** Matthew
**Status:** Proposed. Review before these go into footer/About as permanent brand copy.
**Purpose:** Five short trust statements that define how PuffPrice behaves. Plain English, not corporate. These become footer copy, About-page copy, and the benchmark we hold ourselves to when a product decision is ambiguous.

> **Source basis:** The CLAUDE.md one-rule-above-all ("the experience must work for a real person in a parking lot"), the strategic direction ("Trust is the product"), and the user-persona skepticism patterns (Sarah needs visual trust; Dan disqualifies on any inaccurate data). Written to be durable — short, declarative, not dependent on a specific feature.

---

## The five statements

> 1. **We show what we can verify.** Every deal on PuffPrice was pulled from a dispensary's own menu, website, or a confirmed submission. When we don't have something, we say we don't have it.
>
> 2. **We timestamp what we publish.** Every deal carries the time it was last confirmed. If that timestamp is old, you'll see that too. We don't hide staleness behind a "hot deals" banner.
>
> 3. **We correct fast when we're wrong.** If a deal at the register doesn't match what PuffPrice showed you, email us at hi@puffprice.com. We investigate the same day and update the listing. No forms, no run-around.
>
> 4. **We don't work for dispensaries.** No affiliate links that pay us more for sending you one place over another. No pay-for-placement on the deal list. Featured listings, when they exist, are labeled as paid and still have to match what's on the dispensary menu.
>
> 5. **We don't sell your data.** No account is required to use PuffPrice. The only data we store is what you give us (email for alerts, phone for SMS if you opt in), and we don't sell or share it. Ever.

---

## Where these go

**Footer (all pages).** One-line version or the full five. Recommended one-liner for tight footers: *"We show what we can verify. We timestamp what we publish. We correct fast. We don't sell your data. — The PuffPrice Promise"*

**About page.** Full five-statement block, with a one-sentence intro such as: *"PuffPrice exists to make Illinois cannabis pricing honest. Here's what that means in practice."*

**`/start` page (optional).** Consider surfacing #4 and #5 at the bottom of Section 5 for first-time visitors — those two answer the skeptical Sarah question "is this legit?"

**`/data-sources` page.** Statements #1, #2, and #3 pair naturally with the longer data-sourcing explanation. Reference them rather than re-stating them.

## Voice notes (so future edits don't drift)

- **First person plural ("we").** Not "PuffPrice" in third person. This is a conversation, not corporate speak.
- **Short sentences.** Each statement is one idea. Resist the urge to add qualifiers or legal hedges.
- **Verbs before nouns.** "We show," "We timestamp," "We correct," "We don't work for," "We don't sell." Action-first reads stronger.
- **No "strive to," "aim to," "commit to."** Those are escape hatches. We either do the thing or we don't.
- **No superlatives.** Not "the most trusted," not "Illinois' #1." Those claims erode trust faster than they build it.

---

## Anti-examples — what NOT to write

These are the shapes we specifically reject:

> ~~"PuffPrice is committed to providing Illinois cannabis consumers with the most accurate deal information available in the market."~~ — corporate mush, no verifiable claim.
>
> ~~"We're passionate about transparency 🔥"~~ — emoji + abstraction; reads as marketing.
>
> ~~"Our mission is to empower responsible consumers through data."~~ — mission-statement-ese, meaningless.

Keep it specific. Keep it plain. Keep it short.
