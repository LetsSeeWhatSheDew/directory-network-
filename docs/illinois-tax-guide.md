## H1 and lead

> **# How much does cannabis actually cost in Illinois?**
>
> Not what the shelf says. Illinois cannabis carries one of the highest combined tax rates in the country — roughly **25% in most downstate cities** and **over 26% in Chicago** on standard flower, and up to **41% on high-THC flower and vape cartridges in Cook County**. This guide shows you the layers, the math, and what a real trip to a dispensary costs.

## Section 1 — The four layers of Illinois cannabis tax

Illinois stacks four separate taxes on every adult-use cannabis purchase. All four apply to the same pre-tax shelf price.

### Layer 1: State Cannabis Purchaser Excise Tax

This is the big one, and it's tiered by product type.

| Product | State excise rate |
|---|---|
| Flower / non-infused cannabis with adjusted THC **≤ 35%** | **10%** |
| Flower / non-infused cannabis with adjusted THC **> 35%** | **25%** |
| Cannabis-infused products (edibles, tinctures, drinks, topicals) | **20%** |

One gotcha worth knowing: **vape cartridges and concentrates are NOT "infused products."** They're taxed on the THC scale, and because almost every vape cart on the shelf tests above 35% THC, vapes hit the 25% tier in practice.

### Layer 2: State Retailers' Occupation Tax (general sales tax)

**6.25%** on all adult-use cannabis, on top of the excise above.

### Layer 3: Municipal Cannabis Retailers' Occupation Tax (MCAN)

Illinois cities may impose up to **3%** on adult-use cannabis. Nearly every city with a dispensary — Chicago, Naperville, Aurora, Joliet, Rockford, Peoria, Springfield, Bloomington, Normal, Champaign, Urbana, the Metro East cities — is at the 3% maximum. Rates change in January and July; the most recent change cycle took effect January 1, 2026.

### Layer 4: County Cannabis Retailers' Occupation Tax (CCAN)

Illinois counties may impose up to **3.75% in unincorporated areas** and **3% inside municipalities**. Cook, DuPage, Will, Lake, Kane, Winnebago, Peoria, Sangamon, McLean, Champaign, Madison, and St. Clair counties are all at or near the max where dispensaries operate.

### Plus: general local sales-tax stack

On top of the four cannabis-specific layers, adult-use cannabis is also subject to the **local sales-tax stack** that applies to general merchandise:

- Home Rule Municipal ROT (varies — Chicago: 1.25%)
- County Home Rule ROT (varies — Cook: 1.75%)
- Regional Transportation Authority (RTA) ROT — 1.00% in Cook; 0.75% in DuPage, Kane, Lake, McHenry, Will
- Metro East Transit District — applies in parts of Madison and St. Clair counties

That stack is why the **combined rate** on an adult-use cannabis purchase in Chicago lands at 26.25% on standard flower and **41.25% on high-THC flower** — close to double the effective tax rate of neighboring suburbs.

## Section 2 — The Chicago tax breakdown, line by line

For a ≤35% THC eighth with a $35 shelf price, recreational, purchased in the City of Chicago (Cook County):

| Tax line | Rate | On $35 |
|---|---|---|
| State Cannabis Excise (≤35% THC flower) | 10.00% | $3.50 |
| State Retailers' Occupation Tax | 6.25% | $2.19 |
| Chicago Home Rule ROT | 1.25% | $0.44 |
| Cook County Home Rule ROT | 1.75% | $0.61 |
| RTA Retailers' Occupation Tax | 1.00% | $0.35 |
| Chicago Municipal Cannabis ROT (MCAN) | 3.00% | $1.05 |
| Cook County Cannabis ROT (CCAN) | 3.00% | $1.05 |
| **Total tax** | **26.25%** | **$9.19** |
| **Out-the-door price** | | **$44.19** |

Same math on a $45 vape cartridge (>35% THC), which shifts the excise from 10% to 25%:

| Tax line | Rate | On $45 |
|---|---|---|
| State Cannabis Excise (>35% THC) | 25.00% | $11.25 |
| All other taxes (6.25% + 1.25% + 1.75% + 1% + 3% + 3%) | 16.25% | $7.31 |
| **Total tax** | **41.25%** | **$18.56** |
| **Out-the-door price** | | **$63.56** |

## Section 3 — Chicago vs. downstate — real examples

The same $35 shelf-price, ≤35% THC eighth, purchased recreational:

| City | Combined tax | Out-the-door | Dollars over shelf |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chicago (Cook) | 26.25% | $44.19 | +$9.19 |
| Peoria (Peoria County) | ~25.00% | ~$43.75 | +$8.75 |
| Springfield (Sangamon) | ~24.75% | ~$43.66 | +$8.66 |
| Aurora (Kane) | ~24.50% | ~$43.58 | +$8.58 |
| Naperville (DuPage) | ~24.25% | ~$43.49 | +$8.49 |
| Bloomington (McLean) | ~23.25% | ~$43.14 | +$8.14 |
| Rockford (Winnebago) | ~23.50% | ~$43.23 | +$8.23 |

The downstate discount on standard flower is real but modest: about **$1–$1.50 per eighth** compared to Chicago. On an ounce, about $8–$12. On heavy-monthly-spend patterns ($400–$600/month, like the Dan persona), it compounds to $50–$80 per year — enough to matter, not enough to drive most of the decision.

The bigger driver of out-the-door price variance is **which dispensary** you shop at, not which city. Chain house brands, promotional calendars, and inventory moves swing the price by 20–40% — much more than the 1–3 percentage-point geographic tax variance.

## Section 4 — Medical vs. recreational: the biggest price gap on the shelf

This is the single biggest thing a regular buyer can do to lower their per-purchase cost in Illinois.

**Medical cannabis patients** with an active Illinois medical cannabis card pay:
- **0%** state cannabis excise tax (no tiered 10/20/25% — exempt)
- **1%** state retailers' occupation tax (same as prescription drugs)
- **0%** local cannabis MCAN and CCAN (exempt)
- **0%** local home-rule and county ROT (exempt from most locally-imposed retailers' occupation taxes)
- **1%** RTA Retailers' Occupation Tax where applicable (not exempt)
- **+** Metro East Transit District Retailers' Occupation Tax where applicable (not exempt)

**Effective total for a medical patient in Chicago:** roughly **2%**. That same $35 eighth costs about **$35.70** at the register for a medical patient — versus $44.19 recreational. **A savings of about $8.50 per eighth.**

If you buy cannabis weekly, the card pays for itself inside the first month. Illinois medical card applications run $50 for one year or $100 for two years at the state level, plus physician evaluation. Qualifying conditions are broad; see `illinois.gov/sites/cannabis` for the current list.

This is also why PuffPrice labels medical vs. recreational pricing on every deal page where both are available. They're completely different economies.

## Section 5 — How to calculate your real cost before you go

A simple mental model:

- **Standard flower (≤35% THC), recreational, anywhere in IL:** shelf price × **1.24** (downstate) to **1.27** (Chicago).
- **High-THC flower or vape cart (>35% THC), recreational:** shelf price × **1.39** (downstate) to **1.41** (Chicago).
- **Edibles, drinks, tinctures (infused), recreational:** shelf price × **1.34** (downstate) to **1.36** (Chicago).
- **Anything, medical:** shelf price × **1.02** (plus 1% RTA where applicable).

Rough dollar rules of thumb:
- Every $10 of recreational flower adds about **$2.50–$2.65** in tax.
- Every $10 of recreational edibles adds about **$3.40–$3.60** in tax.
- Every $10 of recreational vape or concentrate adds about **$3.90–$4.15** in tax.

For a precise, address-specific rate, use the [MyTax Illinois Tax Rate Finder](https://mytax.illinois.gov/_/) — it'll return the exact combined cannabis rate for any dispensary's address in the state.

## Section 6 — A simple tax calculator in words

If you want to budget before you leave:

1. Start with the **shelf price** of the item.
2. Add **10%** if it's flower ≤35% THC. Add **25%** if it's flower >35% THC, a vape, or a concentrate. Add **20%** if it's an edible, drink, tincture, or topical.
3. Add **6.25%** for state sales tax.
4. Add **3%** for the city cannabis tax (in any IL city with a dispensary).
5. Add **3%** for the county cannabis tax.
6. Add **1–3%** for the local home-rule sales tax stack (Cook County adds about 3%; most downstate counties add 1–2%).

For standard Chicago flower, that works out to roughly **+26%**. For a high-THC vape in Chicago, roughly **+41%**.

## Section 7 — Why prices vary so much between dispensaries

Tax is only one variable in the gap you see between dispensaries. The bigger drivers:

- **House brand vs. independent wholesale.** Chains that grow their own cannabis (Green Thumb → RISE, Cresco → Sunnyside, PharmaCann → Verilife, Verano → Zen Leaf, Ascend, Curaleaf) sell their in-house products below what independents can match. That's 10–25% baked into the shelf.
- **Promotional calendars.** The same product can be on sale at RISE on Monday, Sunnyside on Wednesday, and Verilife on Friday. None of them is cheaper overall; they're just cheaper on different days.
- **Inventory and end-of-month pressure.** Dispensaries clear inventory on the last few days of the month. This is the single most reliable moment to find a deep discount on top-shelf flower.
- **Local tax differences.** Explained above — worth 1–3 percentage points, or $1–$3 on a $100 basket.
- **Medical vs. recreational menu.** If a dispensary serves both, the medical menu is roughly 25% cheaper at the register for qualifying patients. Mixed-menu dispensaries often look "more expensive" in price comparisons that only pull the rec column.

## Section 8 — What this means for a shopper

- **The shelf price understates your real cost by a quarter to a third.** Budget accordingly.
- **A dollar-savings number is more honest than a percentage.** 30% off a $50 shelf saves you $15 before tax, about $19 at the register in Chicago. 20% off a $75 shelf saves you $15 before tax, about $19 at the register in Chicago. Same dollar save, different percentage.
- **Out-the-door price per gram is the only fair comparison** between two products of different package sizes. An eighth is 3.5g; a quarter is 7g; an ounce is 28g.
- **If you qualify medically, apply.** A $50/year card is the biggest single recurring-cost reduction available to a regular Illinois cannabis buyer.
