## Long description (drop-in for `master_listings.long_description`)

Cookies Chicago is a recreational cannabis dispensary at 215 N Clinton St in the West Loop, about two blocks from Ogilvie Transportation Center and a short walk from the French Market, Union Station, and Fulton Market. The location is inside Chicago proper, which matters for sourcing decisions — under the city's cannabis ordinance, you'll pay Cook County tax (3%) and the Chicago municipal tax (3%) on top of Illinois state cannabis taxes, so product prices ring up meaningfully higher than the same SKUs in a suburban store.

The store carries the Cookies brand line of flower, pre-rolls, vape carts, and edibles — Gelato, Cereal Milk, Pink Rozay, Georgia Pie, and the rest of the catalog — alongside other Illinois-licensed brands. Cookies operates as a chain nationally; the Chicago location is their primary Illinois retail flag. Expect a menu that leans heavier on the Cookies house line than a typical multi-brand dispensary would.

Hours run 9 AM to 9 PM Monday through Saturday and 10 AM to 8 PM Sunday. The location is transit-reachable (Green/Pink Line Clinton; Ogilvie / Union Station for Metra) and has limited street parking — most West Loop visitors walk in from neighboring office buildings, the Metra platforms, or nearby residential. Recreational customers must be 21+ with a valid government-issued photo ID. Medical cardholders can present their IDPH card for tax-advantaged pricing.

Cookies Chicago currently runs a 30% off orders $250+ promotion, which functions as a bulk-buy incentive — the threshold works out to roughly an ounce of mid-tier flower, a couple of vape carts, or a large edible order. This is a stackable-discount rather than a daily deal, so it's worth planning the trip rather than a single-item run.

## FAQs (for `/l/cookies-chicago` detail page)

### Does Cookies Chicago take debit cards?
Illinois dispensaries generally cannot accept traditional credit or debit cards because cannabis remains federally illegal. Most stores accept cashless ATM transactions (debit run as a cash-withdrawal) or have an on-site ATM. Bring cash to avoid fees and the occasional network outage.

### What's parking like in the West Loop near Cookies Chicago?
Street parking around 215 N Clinton is metered and limited, especially during weekday business hours. Paid garages at Ogilvie Transportation Center and 500 W Madison are the closest reliable options. Most visitors arrive via CTA (Green/Pink Line Clinton station) or Metra (Union Station / Ogilvie are both within five minutes on foot).

### Is Cookies Chicago recreational or medical-only?
It's open to both. Adults 21+ with a valid government photo ID can purchase recreational product. Illinois medical cannabis cardholders receive tax advantages (lower state excise taxes) and often get menu priority on limited-allocation products.

### Do they price-match other Illinois dispensaries?
Individual dispensary pricing is not regulated, and Cookies Chicago does not publicly advertise a price-match policy. For lower effective prices on the same SKUs, compare per-gram pricing at suburban dispensaries outside Cook County / Chicago city taxes.

### How does the 30% off $250+ deal actually work?
The discount applies at checkout once your basket reaches $250 before tax. It stacks on top of any first-time-visitor or medical discount the store is running. Cannabis taxes are calculated on the *post-discount* subtotal in Illinois, so the savings hit your final total cleanly.
