# Adjacent Discovery Site Audit
**PuffPrice Competitive Audit — Phase 2**
Audited: 2026-04-28 | Auditor: Claude (Sonnet 4.6)

These sites share PuffPrice's product category (local deal aggregation, discovery tools) but are not cannabis. They define what "good" looks like in the broader vertical.

---

## 2.1 Eater (eater.com)

**URL audited:** https://www.eater.com/

### Hero Section
No hero in the traditional sense — Eater leads with editorial content immediately (top story + video + feed grid). The page is city-organized above the fold. No CTA. Pure content-forward.

### Color Palette
- Background: `#FFFFFF`
- - Brand red: used on the logo wordmark (blocky all-caps serif "EATER" in red)
  - - Text: near-black
    - - Links/accents: red (consistent with brand)
      - - No dominant background color — white canvas
       
        - ### Typography
        - - **Display (headings):** Degular — a geometric with personality, used at 52px for hero article title, tight tracking
          - - **Body/nav:** System sans (`ui-sans-serif` stack)
            - - The Degular choice is important: it's neither generic (Inter) nor cannabis-coded. It signals "editorial voice."
             
              - ### Photography Style
              - Full-bleed editorial food photography on primary story (the "hero"). Thumbnail grid below. Photography is at the center of the value proposition — beautiful, editorial, human. No product shots.
             
              - ### Mobile/Desktop Feel
              - Editorial/magazine-first, responsive. The map integration ("Eater Maps") is a strong trust signal — it anchors content to real-world utility.
             
              - ### Deal/Coupon Presentation
              - Not applicable — Eater doesn't do deals. But the "Best Restaurants" list format (curated, editorially ranked with brief description) is directly applicable to PuffPrice deal card format.
             
              - ### Trust Signals
              - - "Best of" editorial lists (signals taste authority)
                - - Bylines on every piece (accountability)
                  - - City-local focus (signals relevance)
                    - - Map embeds on location-based content
                     
                      - ### About/Story Page
                      - Eater's About page emphasizes editorial voice and city coverage — personality-forward. Not a corporate boilerplate.
                     
                      - ### Visual Register Assessment
                      - Eater proves you can be a **local guide with strong editorial identity without being flashy.** The restraint (white + red + photography) is a lesson. The content IS the design.
                     
                      - **PuffPrice application:** Eater's approach to "Best Restaurants in [City]" maps directly to "Best Deals in Peoria right now." The editorial framing of utility data elevates it from a list to a guide.

                      ## 2.2 The Infatuation (theinfatuation.com)

                      **URL audited:** https://www.theinfatuation.com/

                      ### Hero Section
                      No hero image — leads directly with editorial content grid (3-column, mix of text and photography). Above fold shows: cobalt blue nav bar, "INFATUATION" wordmark in white, "All | CHOOSE CITY" filter row.

                      ### Color Palette
                      - Nav/brand: cobalt blue `#0066F1` — extremely vivid, high contrast
                      - - Background: `#FFFFFF`
                        - - Text: `#000000` (pure black)
                          - - Accent: none beyond the blue
                           
                            - ### Typography
                            - - **Display:** National 2 Condensed at 52px, weight 700, letter-spacing 0.52px — extremely tight condensed serif. The "Oh, British Food Is Cool Now" headline at 52px is visually arresting.
                              - - **Body/UI:** National 2 Narrow, 24px, weight 700 for section heads
                                - - National 2 is a serious typographic investment — it signals editorial authority.
                                 
                                  - ### Photography Style
                                  - All editorial photography — real restaurants, real food, shot beautifully. No stock imagery. No illustration. Photography and text are equal partners.
                                 
                                  - ### Mobile/Desktop Feel
                                  - Desktop-first but clean. The mobile experience likely collapses the 3-column grid to a single feed.
                                 
                                  - ### How They Handle Deals
                                  - The Infatuation doesn't do deals per se, but their "Where to Eat" → "Best [occasion] in [city]" format is structured deal content. The key is **editorial opinion embedded in utility structure.**
                                 
                                  - ### Trust Signals
                                  - - Reviewers have names and personalities
                                    - - City-specific pages and editors
                                      - - Strong opinion voice (the brand IS the opinion)
                                       
                                        - ### Visual Register Assessment
                                        - The Infatuation's `#0066F1` cobalt + National 2 condensed is one of the most distinctive brand systems in the food/local discovery space. It says: "We have a point of view and we're not ashamed of it."
                                       
                                        - **PuffPrice application:** Having a clear brand color (not just green) and a distinctive display font is what separates a directory from a guide. The Infatuation's visual identity makes people trust its opinions before reading a word.

                                        ## 2.3 OpenTable (opentable.com)

                                        **URL audited:** https://www.opentable.com/

                                        ### Hero Section
                                        No traditional hero. The page leads immediately with a utilitarian booking widget: date selector + time selector + party size + search field + "Let's go" CTA. Below that: an AI Concierge section with natural-language suggestion chips ("Show me vegetarian-friendly restaurants", "Find a quiet and intimate place").

                                        ### Color Palette
                                        - Background: `#F1F2F4` (light warm gray)
                                        - - Brand red: `#DA3743` — CTAs, logo, active states
                                          - - Text: dark gray `#1A1A1A`
                                            - - Card backgrounds: white with subtle shadow
                                             
                                              - ### Typography
                                              - - **Full system:** BrandonText — a rounded humanist sans. Used across all heading levels and body. Weight 800 for section headers.
                                                - - The rounded feel of BrandonText softens what is otherwise a very utilitarian interface.
                                                 
                                                  - ### Photography Style
                                                  - Aspirational restaurant photography in card thumbnails. Standard but well-executed. Cards show: photo + restaurant name + cuisine + rating + "Book" CTA.
                                                 
                                                  - ### How They Handle Deal Cards
                                                  - OpenTable doesn't traditionally have deal cards, but their restaurant cards are the benchmark for density: 160x200px image, restaurant name (bold), cuisine type, neighborhood, star rating, review count, availability (dot-matrix time slots). Very high information density without feeling cluttered.
                                                 
                                                  - ### Trust Signals
                                                  - - "Diner's favorite" badges
                                                    - - Star ratings with precise counts
                                                      - - Availability dots (time slots that are bookable NOW)
                                                        - - "1000+ seated" social proof numbers
                                                          - - "Updated on [date]" freshness signal
                                                           
                                                            - ### AI Concierge Feature
                                                            - A 2026 addition: a prominent "Concierge ✦ AI Beta" section with 3 suggested queries and a free-text "Ask a question" input. The background is a soft rainbow gradient (pink-to-teal). This is the most forward-looking UI element in any site audited in Phase 2.
                                                           
                                                            - ### Visual Register Assessment
                                                            - OpenTable reads as **utilitarian-premium.** It's clearly a product company, not a media company. The booking utility is the visual hero. The AI Concierge is a smart addition that future-proofs the UX.
                                                           
                                                            - **PuffPrice application:** OpenTable's restaurant card information density (how much they fit into a card without crowding) is worth directly studying for PuffPrice deal cards. The availability/freshness signals ("Updated April 5") map directly to PuffPrice's deal freshness problem.

                                                            ## 2.4 Resy (resy.com)

                                                            **URL audited:** https://resy.com/

                                                            ### Hero Section
                                                            **The strongest hero of any adjacent site.** Enormous display typography: "Discover Restaurants to Love in Peoria, IL." at ~40px in Beatrice (a contemporary serif) in Resy's signature coral/tomato red (`#E43820` approximately). White background, no photography, no illustration. Pure typography. Below: subtle location-aware booking filters.

                                                            ### Color Palette
                                                            - Primary: coral/tomato `#E43820` — distinctive, memorable, warm
                                                            - - Background: `#FFFFFF`
                                                              - - Text: same coral for headlines, dark gray for body
                                                                - - Nav: white background with coral "RESY" wordmark
                                                                 
                                                                  - ### Typography
                                                                  - - **Display:** Beatrice — a contemporary serif with editorial authority, used at ~40px for hero
                                                                    - - **Body/UI:** GT America — the reliable geometric san used across the industry
                                                                      - - The Beatrice/GT America pairing is sophisticated: editorial display + functional body.
                                                                        - - Letter-spacing on hero: slightly negative on Beatrice, which tightens it
                                                                         
                                                                          - ### Photography Style
                                                                          - Limited on homepage. When photography appears, it's moody restaurant interiors. The design confidence is such that photos aren't needed in the hero.
                                                                         
                                                                          - ### How They Handle Deal/Booking Cards
                                                                          - Restaurant cards: photo (3:2 ratio) + name + neighborhood + cuisine + "Notify Me" or booking button. Very clean. The "Notify Me" waitlist feature is a strong trust signal (we'll tell you when this hot spot has a table).
                                                                         
                                                                          - ### Trust Signals
                                                                          - - "Resy powers the world's best restaurants" — clear authority claim
                                                                            - - Direct booking = trust through utility
                                                                              - - "Resy Spotlight" editorial feature section (curation = editorial authority)
                                                                                - - Bottom-of-page booking filter bar (2 guests / Today / All Day) — persistent utility
                                                                                 
                                                                                  - ### Visual Register Assessment
                                                                                  - **Resy is the benchmark for premium utility design.** It's a booking tool that feels like a luxury brand, not because it uses luxury visual codes (gold, serif everywhere) but because it uses space and typography with total confidence. The coral is bold enough to feel like a brand decision.
                                                                                 
                                                                                  - **PuffPrice application:** Resy proves that a utility product (booking, deals) can have a strong visual identity without sacrificing usability. The Beatrice/GT America pairing — a distinctive serif display + functional san body — is the highest-quality typographic approach in Phase 2.

                                                                                  ## 2.5 Yelp (yelp.com)

                                                                                  **URL audited:** https://www.yelp.com/ (screenshot captured: ss_08325lb4x)

                                                                                  ### Overview (from screenshot only — permission restricted for JS)
                                                                                  Classic Yelp UX: search bar + "Find" CTA leading immediately to local business discovery. Red brand color throughout (`#D32323` approximately). High information density on results pages. Star ratings are the visual backbone.

                                                                                  ### Key Observations
                                                                                  - Yelp's homepage is entirely search-first — no hero photography, no editorial positioning
                                                                                  - - The star rating system (5-star, shown with review count) is the industry-standard trust signal that PuffPrice can't ignore
                                                                                    - - Yelp's map+list hybrid view is the standard layout pattern for local discovery
                                                                                      - - The business card format (photo + stars + review count + category + distance) is information-dense but cluttered by ad placement
                                                                                       
                                                                                        - ### Trust Signals
                                                                                        - - Review count (thousands of reviews = authority)
                                                                                          - - "Claimed" business badges
                                                                                            - - "Verified" markers on photos
                                                                                              - - Open/closed status + hours
                                                                                               
                                                                                                - ### Visual Register Assessment
                                                                                                - Yelp is **functional but not inspiring.** It's the SEO benchmark PuffPrice needs to beat in organic search but not the visual benchmark to aspire to.

                                                                                                ## 2.6 GasBuddy (gasbuddy.com) — The Direct Analog

                                                                                                **URL audited:** https://www.gasbuddy.com/

                                                                                                ### Hero Section
                                                                                                "Check prices faster and save more on gas." — headline in Open Sans at ~28px, weight 700. White background. Below: App Store + Google Play badges with star ratings (380K+ reviews / 770K+ reviews). Single "DOWNLOAD THE APP" full-width CTA in coral-orange.

                                                                                                **This is the closest structural analog to PuffPrice.** GasBuddy does for gas prices exactly what PuffPrice does for cannabis deals: aggregates live prices from a regulated commodity, shows you the best deal nearby, and pushes you to act. Same product category.

                                                                                                ### Color Palette
                                                                                                - Background: `#FFFFFF`
                                                                                                - - Accent: teal `#3BA0C0` (links, nav)
                                                                                                  - - CTA: coral-orange `#E07048`
                                                                                                    - - Logo: teal + orange combination
                                                                                                     
                                                                                                      - ### Typography
                                                                                                      - - **Full system:** Open Sans — the workmanlike system font of 2014-2020. Generic, legible, boring.
                                                                                                        - - No display/body split. Same font everywhere.
                                                                                                          - - This is the "before" state for PuffPrice's typography.
                                                                                                           
                                                                                                            - ### Photography Style
                                                                                                            - Product shots of the app on a phone mockup. Generic phone mockup styling. No lifestyle imagery.
                                                                                                           
                                                                                                            - ### How They Handle Deal Cards
                                                                                                            - GasBuddy's deal cards (gas price comparisons) show: Gas station logo + station name + price + distance + "GasBack" rewards earned. Information-dense but visually flat — white cards, thin borders, small type.
                                                                                                           
                                                                                                            - ### Empty States
                                                                                                            - "No stations found near you" — plain text with a location prompt. No illustration, no helpful guidance.
                                                                                                           
                                                                                                            - ### Trust Signals
                                                                                                            - - 380,000+ App Store reviews (real social proof)
                                                                                                              - - 900M real-time price reports (data credibility)
                                                                                                                - - "No. 1 gas app" positioning claim
                                                                                                                 
                                                                                                                  - ### Visual Register Assessment
                                                                                                                  - **GasBuddy is functionally excellent and visually stale.** The product works. The design hasn't evolved since 2018. It has the information architecture right but the visual treatment is purely utilitarian.
                                                                                                                 
                                                                                                                  - **PuffPrice gap:** PuffPrice is at roughly the same visual register as GasBuddy is today. The visual upgrade needs to close the gap between GasBuddy's current state and where Resy or Dutchie are. The product analogy is perfect; the visual aspiration should be completely different.

                                                                                                                  ## 2.7 Honey/PayPal Honey (joinhoney.com)
                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                  **URL audited:** https://www.joinhoney.com/
                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                  ### Hero Section
                                                                                                                  "We search for coupons at 30,000+ sites to help you save money." — regular-weight (400) headline at 50px in Pangea. Light mint/blue background (`#CDE8E8` approximately). Left: 3D-rendered dollar sign and gift card illustration (floating, no shadow). Right: text + "Add to Chrome — It's Free" CTA in warm orange-brown.
                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                  ### Color Palette
                                                                                                                  - Background: mint/pale teal `#CDE8E8`
                                                                                                                  - - Body: near-white `#FAFAFA`
                                                                                                                    - - Text: dark teal `#1A4A4A`
                                                                                                                      - - CTA: warm orange-brown `#D25C2C`
                                                                                                                        - - Illustration: 3D rendered white plastic objects
                                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                          - ### Typography
                                                                                                                          - - **Display:** Pangea at 50px, weight 400 (not bold — this is a deliberate choice that reads as "confident without trying too hard")
                                                                                                                            - - **Body:** SuisseIntl — a clean Swiss sans used by many premium tech brands
                                                                                                                              - - The Pangea/SuisseIntl pairing is modern and differentiated.
                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                - ### How They Handle Deal Presentation
                                                                                                                                - Honey shows a savings summary ("You saved $X") rather than individual deals. The value proposition is automated/invisible. This is a different model than PuffPrice but the trust-building approach is relevant: Honey shows you proof of savings, not just the promise.
                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                - ### Trust Signals
                                                                                                                                - - "30,000+ stores" (scale claim)
                                                                                                                                  - - App store badges with ratings
                                                                                                                                    - - "Free" emphasized prominently (lowers friction)
                                                                                                                                      - - Savings total visualization (proof-based trust)
                                                                                                                                       
                                                                                                                                        - ### Empty States / No-Data
                                                                                                                                        - Not applicable — Honey works universally. But their handling of the "no coupon found" state is graceful: a friendly illustration + "No coupons found this time, but Honey checked!" message. The empty state maintains brand voice.
                                                                                                                                       
                                                                                                                                        - ### Visual Register Assessment
                                                                                                                                        - Honey reads as **approachable consumer tech.** The mint background + 3D illustration + regular-weight headline creates warmth without being frivolous. The SuisseIntl body font keeps it crisp.
                                                                                                                                       
                                                                                                                                        - **PuffPrice application:** The Pangea at regular weight (not bold) for a large headline is a key lesson — weight doesn't equal authority. The 3D illustration style (plastic/rendered objects) is now widely used and feels contemporary. Most importantly: Honey's "you saved $X" proof-based trust building maps directly to how PuffPrice should frame its deal aggregation value.
