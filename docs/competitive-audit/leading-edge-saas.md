# Leading-Edge Consumer SaaS Visual Audit
**PuffPrice Competitive Audit — Phase 3**
Audited: 2026-04-28 | Auditor: Claude (Sonnet 4.6)

These sites define what "leading-edge 2026" looks like. PuffPrice's visual upgrade should benchmark against these, not against other cannabis sites.

---

## 3.1 Linear (linear.app)

**URL audited:** https://linear.app/

### Hero Section
Jet-black background (`#08090A`). The entire viewport is a single massive headline: "The product development system for teams and agents" — no subhead, no photography, no illustration. Below the headline: two lines of body copy ("Purpose-built for planning and building products. Designed for the AI era.") and a single ghost-link announcement. The bottom third of the first viewport shows a partial screenshot of the Linear product UI, edge-cropped with subtle upward motion on scroll.

### Color Palette
- Background: near-black `#08090A`
- - Body text: off-white `rgb(247, 248, 248)`
  - - Interactive accents: muted purple/violet (used sparingly on icon and CTA)
    - - No secondary color — monochromatic confidence
     
      - ### Typography
      - - **Full system:** Inter Variable at 56px for hero headline, weight 510 (medium — not bold, not light), letter-spacing **-1.232px** (tight, negative tracking)
        - - Body text: Inter Variable at ~16px, normal weight, letter-spacing -0.24px
          - - Section h2: 32px, weight 510, letter-spacing -0.88px
            - - The tight negative tracking is a key Linear signature — it makes standard Inter feel considered and premium
             
              - ### Motion Design
              - - Hero headline appears to fade-up on load (opacity + translateY, subtle ~0.4s)
                - - Product UI screenshot animates in with parallax on scroll
                  - - No decorative animation — motion serves content reveal, not spectacle
                   
                    - ### Whitespace / Breathing Room
                    - Exceptional. The hero section has ~120px vertical padding above the headline. No competing elements. The single-concept-per-viewport rule is followed: hero shows only the product name + positioning.
                   
                    - ### Photography vs Illustration vs Typography
                    - Pure typography-driven hero. Product screenshots below fold. Zero lifestyle photography. Zero illustration. This is unusual and effective — it signals "serious product."
                   
                    - ### What Feels "Expensive"
                    - The tight negative letter-spacing on Inter at this size feels expensive. It's a 2-line change in CSS but it signals typographic intentionality. Most sites use Inter at default tracking and it reads as "developer default" — Linear's tracked Inter reads as "designed."

                    - - ss_6062rmo6i (loading), ss_5744u88gb (loaded hero)

                      ## 3.2 Vercel (vercel.com)

                      **URL audited:** https://vercel.com/home

                      ### Hero Section
                      Pure black background. Center-aligned headline: "Build and deploy on the AI Cloud." at ~26px in Geist (Vercel's own open-source font). Below: subheadline, two CTAs ("Start Deploying" / "Get a Demo"). The hero's visual centerpiece: an animated 3D Vercel triangle logo in the lower half, surrounded by spectral light rays in red/blue/green on a fine grid overlay.

                      ### Color Palette
                      - Background: `#000000`
                      - - Text: near-white `#EDEDEDED`
                        - - Decorative light: full spectrum (red, blue, green light rays emanating from triangle)
                          - - Grid overlay: dark gray lines on black (barely visible, depth-creating)
                           
                            - ### Typography
                            - - **Full system:** Geist — Vercel's own typeface, open-sourced. Clean sans, similar to Inter but with more distinctive character shapes. Used at weight 600 for headlines.
                              - - Letter-spacing: -1.701px on hero headline (tighter than Linear)
                                - - This is a notable move: building and releasing your own font is a significant brand investment.
                                 
                                  - ### Motion Design
                                  - - The 3D triangle rotates slowly on the z-axis (continuous, subtle)
                                    - - Light rays animate with a breathing/pulsing effect
                                      - - Tab switching below fold reveals different product categories with animated content transitions
                                       
                                        - ### Whitespace
                                        - Extremely generous — the 3D triangle occupies almost the bottom half of the hero viewport, creating breathing room above the text.
                                       
                                        - ### Photography vs Illustration vs Typography
                                        - Zero photography. The 3D animated logo IS the hero visual. Below fold: dark-mode product screenshots, technical diagrams.
                                       
                                        - ### What Feels "Expensive"
                                        - The animated 3D triangle with spectral lighting. This is not a static image — it's a real-time rendered 3D element. Plus the custom typeface. Both signal significant design investment.

                                                                      ## 3.5 Anthropic (anthropic.com)

                                                                      **URL audited:** https://www.anthropic.com/

                                                                      ### Hero Section
                                                                      **The most refined hero of all sites audited.** Warm linen off-white background (`#FAF9F5`). Left-aligned headline: "AI research and products that put safety at the frontier" — in Anthropic Sans at ~46px, weight 700. The words "research" and "products" have typographic underline-emphasis treatment. Body copy in Anthropic Serif at 18px (the body font is a custom serif — unusual for a tech company). No photography, no illustration in the hero.

                                                                      ### Color Palette
                                                                      - Background: warm off-white `#FAF9F5` (linen/parchment)
                                                                      - - Text: near-black `#141413`
                                                                        - - No brand accent color on the homepage — zero color used decoratively
                                                                          - - Black rectangle cards below fold (for product/research sections)
                                                                           
                                                                            - ### Typography
                                                                            - - **Hero headline:** Anthropic Sans, ~46px, weight 700 — custom humanist sans, similar to Aktiv Grotesk but warmer
                                                                              - - **Body:** Anthropic Serif, ~18px, weight 400 — custom slab/oldstyle serif. Using a serif for body is an emphatic editorial choice.
                                                                                - - **Article titles:** Anthropic Serif at 70px, weight 400 (regular weight at huge size — confident)
                                                                                  - - Custom font investment signals: "We thought about every typographic decision."
                                                                                   
                                                                                    - ### Motion Design
                                                                                    - Subtle. Content sections appear with fade-in on scroll. No decorative animation. Motion is purely for content reveal.
                                                                                   
                                                                                    - ### Whitespace
                                                                                    - Maximum whitespace. The hero has ~160px above the headline. Below the hero: a black card (full width, dark contrast) creates a dramatic moment-of-pause before the content continues. This black card is the best single design element on the page.
                                                                                   
                                                                                    - ### Photography vs Illustration vs Typography
                                                                                    - Zero photography on hero. Zero illustration. The full-width black card below the fold contains embedded video/interactive content. Typography IS the design.
                                                                                   
                                                                                    - ### What Feels "Expensive"
                                                                                    - Everything. Custom fonts. The linen background (not pure white — this is a deliberate, expensive-feeling choice). The full-width black card. The unlined editorial underline treatment on key words. The 70px regular-weight serif for article titles.
                                                                                   
                                                                                    - ### PuffPrice Application
                                                                                    - The warm off-white background (`#FAF9F5` vs PuffPrice's `#F5F4F0`) and the typography-first hero are the most directly transferable elements. PuffPrice's Georgia serif hero is already in the Anthropic spirit — it needs more scale, more negative tracking, and more whitespace to get there.

                                                                                                                                        ## Cross-Site Observations: What "Leading-Edge 2026" Actually Looks Like
                                                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                                        ### 1. Custom or Variable Fonts with Tight Negative Tracking
                                                                                                                                        Linear (-1.23px), Vercel (-1.70px), Arc (-1.82px), Anthropic (normal — the exception) all use tight negative tracking at display sizes. This single CSS property separates designed from undesigned.
                                                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                                        ### 2. Near-Black or Warm Off-White Backgrounds (Not Pure White or Pure Black)
                                                                                                                                        Linear: `#08090A`. Vercel: `#000000`. Anthropic: `#FAF9F5`. Arc: `#FFFCEC`. Pure white backgrounds are for commodity interfaces. Near-black or warm white signals intentionality.
                                                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                                        ### 3. One Concept Per Viewport
                                                                                                                                        Linear, Resy, Anthropic, and Arc all apply the rule: one idea per full viewport. No content stacking below the fold within the same visual section.
                                                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                                        ### 4. Motion Is Content-Serving, Not Decorative
                                                                                                                                        Every site that uses motion (Linear, Vercel, Arc, Figma) uses it to reveal content or demonstrate product. No particle effects, no background animations for their own sake.
                                                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                                        ### 5. Information Architecture That Decreases Density as You Scroll Down
                                                                                                                                        All leading sites front-load the single most important thing (the headline) and increase density as the user scrolls. Cannabis sites do the opposite: ads + navigation + categories above the fold = overwhelming.
                                                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                                        ### 6. Photography Ratio
                                                                                                                                        - Linear: 0% photography
                                                                                                                                        - - Vercel: 0% photography
                                                                                                                                          - - Anthropic: 0% photography
                                                                                                                                            - - Figma: 100% customer work (≠ photography)
                                                                                                                                              - - Arc: product screenshots
                                                                                                                                                - - Notion: illustration
                                                                                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                  - The trend is away from stock or lifestyle photography toward product UI, customer work, or pure typography.
