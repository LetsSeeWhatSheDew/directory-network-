## Template — Every City Page Gets These Six Pieces

1. **Opening answer line** — the first sentence below the H1. Answer-formatted, pulls from live data. This is what gets featured-snipped and AI-cited.
2. **Cannabis scene blurb (2-3 sentences)** — why cannabis matters in this city. Adds context for humans and entities for search.
3. **Neighborhood keyword set (3-5)** — for internal linking, schema, and long-tail capture.
4. **Dispensary clusters** — where the stores actually are. Helps with "near me" routing and neighborhood pages later.
5. **Local landmarks** — for directions context ("across from Grant Park," "minutes from O'Hare," "off I-74 near Junction City"). Humanizes the page and helps out-of-state visitors who don't know street names.
6. **City-specific FAQ additions** — 1-2 FAQs beyond the template, unique to this city.

## 1. Chicago

**URL:** `/deals/chicago` (with future neighborhood pages at `/deals/chicago/[neighborhood]`)
**Page title:** `Chicago Dispensary Deals — PuffPrice`
**H1:** `Dispensary Deals in Chicago, Illinois`

### Opening answer line (live-data)
> **"[X] dispensaries in Chicago, IL are running [Y] active deals right now, updated [date]. Browse every deal on the map below and hit **GO HERE** to get directions in one tap."**

Replace `[X]`, `[Y]`, `[date]` at render from the deals view. Worst case fallback copy when live data is zero: "Chicago dispensary deals refresh throughout the day — check back for today's offers."

### Cannabis scene blurb (2-3 sentences)
Chicago has more licensed dispensaries than any other city in Illinois — roughly 50+ locations across downtown, the North Side, the South Side, and the far-flung neighborhoods. The Illinois cannabis market is one of the most expensive in the country, with eighths typically $45-65 and a combined tax burden that can top 40% inside Cook County, so deal hunting is how most Chicagoans actually afford the high-end product. PuffPrice ranks every active Chicago deal by savings, freshness, and distance — so a 25%-off flower deal at a Wicker Park shop surfaces ahead of a "first-time customer" throwaway at a chain you'll never walk into.

### Neighborhood keyword set (use for internal links, schema, and future micro-pages)
- **River North** — flagship dispensary district; Verilife River North, Sunnyside River North, Green Rose are all here
- **Wicker Park / West Town** — nuEra, NuMed, Spark'd, MOCA — dense and walkable
- **South Loop** — Prairie Cannabis, Dispensary 33-adjacent; border of downtown and the museum campus
- **Lincoln Park** — emerging (Karma Club, planned Ümi at former Salt 'n Pepper Diner site)
- **Lakeview** — university-adjacent, strong student demand
- **Near North / Magnificent Mile** — tourist-facing; Guaranteed Dispensary area
- **Logan Square / Humboldt Park** — growing selection
- **South Shore / South Chicago** — Mission Dispensaries, equity-license growth area
- **O'Hare / Far Northwest** — travelers and workers; higher-traffic pickup demand
- **Evanston border** — neighborhood flow into Lincoln Park and Rogers Park

### Dispensary clusters
- **Downtown / River North corridor** — densest cluster; most out-of-state visitor traffic
- **West Town / Wicker Park** — walkable cluster, best neighborhood for deal-hopping on foot
- **South Loop / Museum Campus** — tourist spillover, moderate density
- **North Side (Lakeview → Rogers Park)** — spread out, tied to transit lines
- **South Side** — spread out, car-dependent, equity-license growth

### Local landmarks for directions context
- **Magnificent Mile** (shopping district — Verilife is blocks from it)
- **Grant Park / Millennium Park** (museum campus, near downtown dispensaries — Cookies Chicago cites "dispensary near Grant Park")
- **Willis Tower / Sears Tower** (anchor for Loop directions)
- **Wrigley Field** (Lakeview dispensary anchor)
- **O'Hare and Midway airports** (tourists need nearby pickup options)
- **CTA Red Line / Blue Line** (rail access for transit riders)
- **I-90, I-94, I-290** (car arrival — Verilife River North is "accessible via I-90/94")

### City-specific FAQ additions
- **Q: Which Chicago neighborhood has the most dispensaries?**
  A: River North has the highest concentration of dispensary storefronts in the city, with multiple flagship locations from chains like Verilife, Sunnyside, and Green Rose within walking distance of each other.
- **Q: Can I buy cannabis in Chicago if I live in another state?**
  A: Yes. Out-of-state adults 21+ can purchase up to 15 grams of flower, 250mg of edible THC, and 2.5 grams of concentrate per day in Illinois — half the limit for Illinois residents. Bring a valid government-issued photo ID.
- **Q: Why are Chicago dispensary prices higher than the Illinois average?**
  A: Cook County adds a local cannabis tax on top of the state's 10–25% tax, and Chicago's city tax further lifts the total tax burden above 40% on some products. Watch for BOGO and % off deals — they're the only reliable way to beat the tax stack.

## 2. Peoria

### Cannabis scene blurb (2-3 sentences)
Peoria is Central Illinois' cannabis hub — about 10 dispensaries serve the city and surrounding Tri-County region, including independents like Ivy Hall (opened December 2023 on War Memorial Drive) and chain locations from Sunnyside, Verilife, and others. Because Peoria draws from rural counties where retail cannabis is sparse, its dispensaries often run aggressive first-time discounts and loyalty promotions to capture shoppers who may drive in from Tazewell, Woodford, and Knox counties. PuffPrice tracks every active Peoria deal in real time, ranked so the sharpest savings surface first.

### Dispensary clusters

  A: East Peoria has its own dispensary cluster ~10 min east over the Murray Baker Bridge. Bloomington dispensaries are ~40 min northeast on I-74. Pekin has 1-2 dispensaries ~15 min south.

## 3. Rockford

**URL:** `/deals/rockford`
**Page title:** `Rockford Dispensary Deals — PuffPrice`
**H1:** `Dispensary Deals in Rockford, Illinois`

### Opening answer line (live-data)
> **"[X] dispensaries in Rockford, IL are running [Y] active deals right now. Rockford is the closest legal recreational market for much of southern Wisconsin — Sunnyside, Lyfe, and Mapleglen are all a 30-minute drive from Beloit."**

### Cannabis scene blurb (2-3 sentences)
Rockford is the largest city in northern Illinois outside Chicago and the nearest legal-cannabis destination for a huge stretch of southern Wisconsin — including Beloit, Janesville, and Madison shoppers where recreational cannabis remains illegal. That cross-border draw makes Rockford deals consistently competitive; first-time discounts for WI residents are common and BOGO pricing shows up most weeks. Local operators like Lyfe (on Troy Drive off East State) join chain stores like Sunnyside and Mapleglen Care Center in the East State / Perryville corridor.

### Neighborhood keyword set
- **East State Street corridor (US-20)** — primary retail spine and dispensary artery
- **Perryville Road** — parallel north-south retail corridor; anchor for Sunnyside Rockford (2696 McFarland Rd)
- **Downtown Rockford** — riverfront redevelopment zone
- **Rockford west side** — less dense, complement to east-side cluster
- **Loves Park** — northern suburb, spillover dispensary traffic

### Dispensary clusters
- **East State / Perryville corridor** — the anchor; Lyfe (6774 Troy Drive), Sunnyside Rockford, Mapleglen Care Center all accessible from here
- **Downtown / riverfront** — smaller cluster, tied to the Rock River district

### Local landmarks for directions context
- **East State Street (US-20)** — the primary retail artery and direction anchor
- **Perryville Road** — parallel north-south retail spine
- **Cherryvale Mall** (east side mall, natural dispensary clustering reference)
- **Rock River / downtown riverfront**
- **I-90 / I-39 / US-20 interchange** (Wisconsin arrivals)
- **BMO Center / Indoor Sports Center** (event anchor)
- **Rockford Art Museum / Coronado Theatre** (downtown cultural anchors)
- **Beloit, Wisconsin border** (~15 minutes north — primary cross-border corridor)

### City-specific FAQ additions
- **Q: Can Wisconsin residents buy cannabis at Rockford dispensaries?**
  A: Yes. Rockford is a ~30-minute drive from Beloit, WI, and Wisconsin residents 21+ can buy up to 15 grams of flower, 250mg of edible THC, and 2.5 grams of concentrate per visit (half the Illinois-resident limit). Bring your driver's license; you'll pay Illinois sales + cannabis tax.
- **Q: Which Rockford dispensary has the best deals for first-time customers?**
  A: All Rockford dispensaries offer first-time discounts, typically 15-25% off. PuffPrice's Rockford page ranks today's biggest first-time offers at the top — filter by "new customer" to see them.

## 4. Springfield

**URL:** `/deals/springfield`

### Cannabis scene blurb (2-3 sentences)
Springfield is Illinois' state capital and central-Illinois government hub, and its cannabis retail reflects that — dispensaries here draw from a 40-mile rural radius of Sangamon County residents, government workers, and travelers visiting Lincoln-era landmarks. HCI Alternatives (now operating under the Ascend brand after merging in 2018) occupies a prominent downtown spot at 628 East Adams Street — steps from the Old State Capitol — while newer arrivals like High Profile Cannabis anchor the south side. Prices run slightly below Chicago because Sangamon County doesn't stack the same local tax load as Cook County.

### Dispensary clusters
- **Downtown Adams Street** — anchor cluster around the capitol
- **South side (MacArthur / Wabash)** — suburban cluster
- **East side (Dirksen)** — smaller cluster serving commuters

### Local landmarks for directions context

- **Lincoln's Tomb / Oak Ridge Cemetery**

- **Memorial Medical Center / HSHS St. John's** (hospital district, pedestrian traffic)

### City-specific FAQ additions

  A: HCI Alternatives / Ascend Springfield at 628 East Adams Street is within a 5-minute walk of the Old State Capitol and about 10 minutes from the current state capitol building.
- **Q: Are there dispensaries near Lincoln's historic sites?**
  A: Yes. Downtown Springfield dispensaries on Adams Street are within a 10-minute walk of the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library & Museum, the Old State Capitol, and Lincoln's home.

## 5. Aurora

**URL:** `/deals/aurora`
**Page title:** `Aurora Dispensary Deals — PuffPrice`
**H1:** `Dispensary Deals in Aurora, Illinois`

### Opening answer line (live-data)
> **"[X] dispensaries in Aurora, IL and neighboring North Aurora have [Y] active deals right now. Verilife North Aurora on Orchard Road and nuEra Aurora are the primary anchors — both are off I-88."**

### Cannabis scene blurb (2-3 sentences)
Aurora is the second-largest city in Illinois by population (~180K), but its dispensary count is surprisingly low — most searches for "Aurora dispensary" actually route to nearby North Aurora or Naperville. Verilife North Aurora on Orchard Road (just off I-88) and nuEra Aurora anchor the area, drawing from Aurora, Oswego, Sugar Grove, and Naperville-west. Because the retail density is thin relative to population, deals here compete less on depth (not much undercutting) and more on loyalty; PuffPrice surfaces every live offer so you don't end up driving to North Aurora for a deal you could've gotten closer in Naperville.

### Neighborhood keyword set
- **North Aurora** — Verilife flagship (2080 W. Orchard Rd.)
- **Downtown Aurora / Fox River district** — revitalized district, Route 25 corridor
- **East Aurora / Farnsworth Avenue** — east-side retail corridor
- **West Aurora** — university-adjacent (Aurora University)
- **Montgomery / Oswego** — adjacent suburbs, dispensary-relevant for nearby driving

### Dispensary clusters
- **North Aurora / Orchard Road** — primary cluster, I-88 access
- **Route 25 / Fox River corridor** — Aurora city proper, still developing
- **Adjacent Naperville dispensaries (~15 min east)** — realistic driving option for west-Naperville and east-Aurora shoppers

### Local landmarks for directions context
- **I-88 / Orchard Road exit** (primary Verilife North Aurora route)
- **I-88 / Farnsworth Avenue exit** (east Aurora)
- **Fox River / Route 25** (natural north-south corridor)
- **Hollywood Casino Aurora** (downtown anchor)
- **Paramount Theatre** (downtown cultural reference)
- **Naperville downtown (~25 min east)** (neighbor-city anchor)
- **Aurora University / Aurora Municipal Airport** (neighborhood anchors)
- **Chicago Premium Outlets (in Aurora)** — huge retail anchor that also generates cannabis search

### City-specific FAQ additions
- **Q: Why does Aurora have so few dispensaries compared to its population?**
  A: Aurora is the second-largest city in Illinois but has fewer dispensaries than smaller cities like Peoria or Joliet. Most of the Aurora-area cannabis retail is concentrated in North Aurora on Orchard Road or in nearby Naperville. Expect this to change as more conditional licenses become operational.
- **Q: Is North Aurora a separate city from Aurora?**
  A: Yes. North Aurora is a separate village immediately north of Aurora. Verilife North Aurora (2080 W. Orchard Rd.) is technically outside Aurora city limits but is the closest major dispensary for most Aurora residents — under 10 minutes from downtown Aurora.

## Implementation Notes for Code

### Live-data variables to wire into each opening line
- `activeDealsCount` — count of deals where `active = true` and `city = [city]`
- `dispensaryCount` — count of distinct dispensaries with at least one active deal in city
- `lastUpdatedTimestamp` — most recent deal insert/update in city; surface as "Updated [date]" in human-readable form

### Schema to apply
- `Place` or `City` schema on each city page (establishes the geographic entity)
- `ItemList` wrapping the deal cards
- `FAQPage` for the FAQ section
- `BreadcrumbList` for Home > Illinois > [City]
- On each individual deal card: `SpecialAnnouncement` with `datePosted`, `expires`, `announcementLocation`

### Fallback copy (zero active deals in a city)
> "No active deals in [City] right now — but this page updates multiple times a day as dispensaries post new offers. Set up a **free deal alert** and we'll notify you when a [City] deal goes live."

### Internal link pattern
Each city page should link to:
- Illinois parent page (`/deals/illinois`)
- 2-3 neighboring cities (e.g., Chicago → Naperville, Aurora, Evanston)
- Any neighborhood micro-pages once built

### Images / visual anchors
- Hero image per city: a recognizable skyline or landmark (Chicago skyline, Peoria Riverfront, Rockford's Anderson Japanese Gardens, Springfield Capitol Dome, Aurora Paramount Theatre)
- Map embed centered on the city, showing active deal pin density
