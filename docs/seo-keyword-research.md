## The Keyword Landscape in One Paragraph

Cannabis search in Illinois is dominated by three intents: **"where do I buy"** (high volume, transactional), **"what's on sale"** (medium volume, highest purchase intent), and **"is it legal to..."** (high volume, educational). The "where do I buy" territory is locked up by Weedmaps, Leafly, Yelp, and dispensary Google Business Profiles — hard to crack. The **"what's on sale"** territory is soft: Weedmaps and Leafly rank generically but nobody owns the Illinois state-specific deal queries. That's PuffPrice's lane. The **educational / statistical** territory ("how many dispensaries in Illinois," "cheapest dispensary in Chicago," "when do dispensaries run deals") is where Zone 4 content plays — AI citations and featured snippets.

## 3. Competitive Ranking Landscape

### Who ranks for "dispensary deals chicago" today (top 10 SERP)
- Weedmaps (owns `/deals/united-states/illinois/chicago`)
- Leafly (owns `/deals/chicago-il-us`)
- Cannasaver / CannaPages
- do312.com (local Chicago events site)
- Individual dispensary chain pages (Verilife, RISE, Sunnyside)
- News/blog roundups ("Best 420 deals in Chicago")

### Why PuffPrice can break the top 5 in 90 days
- URL slug match: `/deals/chicago` is semantic
- Freshness signal: 56 active deals updated in real time
- Answer-format opening: "X active deals at Y Chicago dispensaries right now"
- Schema markup: SpecialAnnouncement on each deal, LocalBusiness on each listing
- Internal linking: each city page links to its state parent and neighbor cities
- No ad gunk / paid placement, so UX signals (dwell time, scroll depth) should be strong

### Competitor weaknesses we exploit
- **Weedmaps and Leafly** don't re-render their "X deals active" counts dynamically — they're generic. Our page literally counts.
- **Cannasaver** has stale data, weak Core Web Vitals, and a dated UI. We beat them on everything except domain authority.
- **Chain sites** (Verilife / RISE / Sunnyside) can only speak for their own deals. They never beat an aggregator for "cheapest dispensary in [city]" queries.
- **do312.com** is an events/lifestyle site, not a structured data source. Loses to a well-structured deal page.

## 6. Content Format Recommendations by Query Type

### For **"[city] dispensary deals"** queries
- URL: `/deals/[city]`
- H1: "Dispensary Deals in [City], IL"
- Above the fold: answer line "X active deals at Y [city] dispensaries — updated [date]."
- Below: deal cards ranked by score
- Schema: SpecialAnnouncement + BreadcrumbList + ItemList

### For **"how many dispensaries in [state/city]"** queries
- URL: `/illinois/dispensary-count` or similar
- H1: "How Many Dispensaries Are in Illinois?"
- First line: the answer. Full stop.
- Below: IDFPR attribution, license breakdown, city distribution, update timestamp
- Schema: FAQ + Article

### For **"cheapest dispensary in [city]"** queries
- URL: `/deals/[city]/cheapest`
- H1: "Cheapest Dispensary Deals in [City], IL"
- First line: "The [N]%-off deals at [dispensary] are currently the biggest discounts in [city]."
- Below: ranked list of biggest-% deals currently active
- Schema: ItemList + FAQ

### For **"what day has the best weed deals"** queries
- URL: `/illinois/best-day-for-deals`
- H1: "What Day of the Week Has the Best Cannabis Deals in Illinois?"
- First line: "Monday." (Then the 200-word explanation.)
- Table of deal frequency by day, sourced from our own 56-deal dataset
- Schema: FAQ + Article

### For **seasonal** queries ("4/20 deals," "black friday cannabis," "greens day")
- URL: `/il/4-20-deals` (year-agnostic, updated annually)
- H1: "Illinois 4/20 Deals [Year]"
- First line: "[N] dispensaries are running 4/20 deals in Illinois, through [date]."
- Below: real deals ranked by savings
- Update annually; keep prior-year content redirected if we swap URLs

## Takeaways for Matthew

**1. The quick-win opportunity is city pages with answer-format openings.** Every city page needs "X active deals at Y dispensaries in [city] right now" as the first line below the H1. This single change unlocks featured snippets.

**2. The Zone 4 play is in the People Also Ask column.** Twenty-six questions above. Each is a capturable page. The 10 highest-priority ones can be built as standalone pages or rolled into an FAQ hub.

**3. Deal-type taxonomy is a free SEO layer.** "BOGO," "first-time," "veteran," "medical," "happy hour" — each becomes a filterable landing page. Low effort, capturable volume, rank-forever potential.

**4. Our biggest competitive leverage is Illinois-specificity + freshness.** We can't out-authority Weedmaps on generic terms; we can out-specific them on every IL-modified query.

**5. The single most rankable keyword in 90 days is likely "how many dispensaries in illinois"** — zero competition, PuffPrice has the data infrastructure to answer it authoritatively, and the answer is a stepping stone to AI citation.
